Key Events

In The Sand Creek Massacre of 1864 U.S. Troops attacted a peacefull Cheyenne and Arapaho Camp in Colorado, Killing lots of women and children. This bloody event caused much national outrage and government investigation but also increased military control over the indians rather than protection for native Americans

In the Great Sioux War (1865-1867): plains tribes fought against U.S. expansion along the Boxeman Trail. This conflict was costly for the government and it resulted in temporary treaties such as the Treaty of Fort Laramie, which although briefly recognized Sioux land rights.

These events had a significant impact on U.S. policy toward natives as it set a standard for the U.S. to rely heavily on military force in order to force tribes to set treaties to clear land for settlers. These events also helped establish the reservation system and long term federal control over Native American life.
