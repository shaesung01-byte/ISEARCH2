Key Events

Sand Creek Massacre (1864): U.S. troops attacked a peaceful Cheyenne and Arapaho camp in Colorado, killing many women and children. The event caused national outrage and a government investigation, but it led to increased military control rather than protection for Native peoples.

Great Sioux War (1865â€“1867): Plains tribes fought to defend their land against U.S. expansion along the Bozeman Trail. The conflict was costly for the government and resulted in temporary treaties, like the Treaty of Fort Laramie, which briefly recognized Sioux land rights.

Impact on Government Policy: These events influenced U.S. policy toward Native nations, relying more on military force and using treaties to clear land for settlers. They helped establish the reservation system and long-term federal control over Native affairs.
