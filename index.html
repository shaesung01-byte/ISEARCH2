Background

The sand creek massacare (1864) and the Great Sioux war (1865-1867) revealed the growing tension between the westward expansion of the U.S. and Native American sovereignty. In the Sand Creek Massacare, U.S. troops killed hundreds of Cheyenne and Arapaho people, which shocked the nation when the new leaked and led to a congressional investigation. In the investigation although only a few U.S. officials were reprimanded the event revealed the failures of federal policy towards natives.

Unfortunately instead of lasting change in the following years the U.S. begain to increasingly rely on military force in order to forcibly take land from natives instead of peacibly coming to settlements or coeixisting with them to get land for settlment and railroads. Additionally, even after the U.S. would force the natives to sign unfair treaties with military pressure they would often go back on these treaties to get land.
